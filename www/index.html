Hello there!

Nepalese Young Professionals in New York (NYPNY) is a social group for Nepalese professionals residing in the NY/NJ/CT area, who come together for three primary objectives.

    Connecting people, forming relationships and generating opportunities through Networking.
    Fostering mentorship within the connected community.
    Empowering professionals for entrepreneurship and professional development so they can be the next generation business and community leaders.

We are working on creating an active community of doers, so why don't you join and find more about our events and ongoings on our Facebook Page, or join discussions on our Discussion Forum.

CAMPAIGN

You might already be aware of the devastating earthquake that hit Nepal in the morning of April 25. Until now more than 5,000 people have been confirmed dead, and the number is increasing. The UN estimates that 8 million people have been affected and 2.8 million people have been displaced from their homes. UN also estimates that 3.5 million people will be in need of food assistance. That is a lot of people trying to fulfill just their basic needs. We may not share the victim’s anguish but we can definitely help to alleviate it.

The Nepali government is doing what it can with limited resources and infrastructure. Organizations like Red Cross, Oxfam, CARE, etc are also on the ground with supplies and volunteers. But it is no match to the devastation the earthquake has left behind.

Along with the big organizations, many local groups have also risen to the occasion and shown incentives to make a difference. Nepalese Young Professionals in New York ("NYPNY"), are collaborating with credible local youth groups who have volunteered to go to remote parts of the country to provide relief. NYPNY believes that this is the best method to ensure that the real victims get relief in time.

At this time 25 such groups have signed up to receive funds from us. NYPNY has graded each of these local groups on the basis of 6 broad categories. They are task/fund fit, accountability, response time, location of project, category, and cost per person. Each project is headed by a project overseer who will be assisting and communicating with these local groups on a regular basis. To make the process as transparent as possible, we will be posting the updates of each campaign in our website.

NYPNY request your contribution in this moment of need. We are partnering with Grass Root Movement in Nepal (GMIN), a 501 (C) 3 registered nonprofit organization in the USA, for this fund raise. (More info on GMIN at http://www.gmin.us). 100% of the donations raised from this campaign will be donated to local organizations working directly with the victims in Nepal.
